FAQ'S

What is life insurance?

Life insurance is a contract between an individual and an insurance company, where the insurer promises to pay a specified amount of money to the designated beneficiaries upon the insured person's death.

Why do I need life insurance?

Life insurance provides financial protection for your loved ones in the event of your death. It can help replace lost income, cover funeral expenses, pay off debts, and ensure your family's financial stability.

How much life insurance coverage do I need?

The amount of life insurance coverage you need depends on various factors, including your income, debts, lifestyle, and financial goals. It is recommended to assess your financial situation and consult with a financial advisor to determine the appropriate coverage amount.

Can I change my life insurance policy?

Yes, life insurance policies can often be modified or changed. You can typically increase or decrease coverage, convert term insurance to permanent insurance, or make changes to beneficiaries. Contact your insurance provider for specific details.

What happens if I stop paying premiums?

If you stop paying premiums, your life insurance policy may lapse or be canceled. The coverage will no longer be in effect, and your beneficiaries will not receive a death benefit. Some policies may have a grace period or options to convert the policy to a reduced paid-up policy or extended term insurance.

How do I file a life insurance claim?

To file a life insurance claim, you will need to contact the insurance company and provide them with the necessary documents, such as a death certificate and the policy information. The insurance company will guide you through the claims process.

Is a medical exam required to obtain life insurance?

In many cases, a medical exam is required to obtain life insurance, especially for larger coverage amounts. The exam helps the insurance company assess the applicant's health and determine the risk. However, some policies, such as simplified issue or guaranteed issue policies, may not require a medical exam but may have limitations or higher premiums.

Can I have multiple life insurance policies?

Yes, it is possible to have multiple life insurance policies. Many people opt for multiple policies to provide additional coverage or to have different types of coverage, such as a combination of term and whole life insurance.
